Healthcare Patient & Admission Analytics 

Project Overview
    This project focuses on analyzing healthcare patient and hospital admission data using SQL and Power BI. The analysis was performed to identify patterns in patient demographics, diagnoses, hospital admissions, obesity trends, allergies, and specialist coverage. Interactive dashboards and analytical reports were created to transform raw healthcare data into actionable business insights for healthcare management and operational decision-making.

 Objectives
     1. Analyze patient demographics based on gender and age group
     2. Identify the most common diagnoses and admission trends
     3. Study obesity distribution among patients
     4. Analyze allergy patterns and healthcare risk indicators
     5. Evaluate hospital admission trends across quarters
     6. Generate business recommendations for healthcare optimization

Key Insights
1. Patient Demographics
      Male patients accounted for a slightly higher percentage of admissions compared to female patients
      Patients born in the 1990s represented the majority of hospital admissions
      The dataset included a broad patient age range spanning multiple decades

2. Diagnosis Analysis
      Congestive Heart Failure and Pregnancy were among the most common diagnoses
      Cardiovascular-related conditions contributed significantly to hospital admissions
      Multiple patients showed repeated admissions for similar diagnoses, indicating chronic condition patterns

3. Obesity & Health Risk Analysis
      Obesity indicators were calculated using BMI formulas
      Certain demographic groups showed higher obesity prevalence
      Obesity trends highlighted potential preventive healthcare opportunities

4. Admission Trends
      Admissions peaked during Quarter 3
      Seasonal admission patterns suggested varying hospital resource demands
      Same-day admissions and discharges indicated efficient handling for certain cases

5. Allergy Analysis
      "NKA" (No Known Allergies) was the most common allergy status
       Penicillin and Codeine were among the most frequently reported allergies
       Accurate allergy documentation remains critical for patient safety

Business Recommendations

1. Strengthen Preventive Healthcare Programs: Develop obesity awareness and preventive care initiatives to reduce long-term health complications and hospital admissions.
2. Improve Cardiovascular Care Programs: Expand cardiac care services and screening programs to address the high number of cardiovascular-related diagnoses.
3. Optimize Hospital Resource Allocation: Use seasonal admission trends to improve staffing, scheduling, and resource planning during peak admission periods.
4. Enhance Allergy Documentation Systems: Improve allergy data collection and monitoring systems to support safer medication administration and treatment planning.
5. Monitor Repeat Admissions: Track patients with repeated admissions to identify chronic disease risks and improve long-term patient management strategies.
